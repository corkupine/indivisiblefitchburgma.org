5 Things You Can Do

Taking action to protect democracy

Make a Monthly Donation

Recurring donations help organizations plan ahead and stay strong for the long fight. Even $5/month makes a difference

  • ACLU – defends civil liberties, voting rights, reproductive freedom, and immigrant protections
  • Common Cause – champions fair elections and getting money out of politics
  • RAICES – provides legal services and advocacy for immigrants and asylum seekers
  • VoteVets – empowers veterans and holds politicians accountable
  • LUCE Mass – coalition of immigrant-led, grassroots organizations who build people power for everyone across Massachusetts

Use the 5 Calls App

The 5 Calls app makes it easy to contact your elected officials. It provides phone numbers, scripts, and issue summaries so you can make quick, effective calls about the issues that matter most. Calling is one of the most impactful ways to influence your representatives—this app helps you do it in minutes.

Visit 5calls.org

Join a Local Pro-Democracy Group

Change starts at the local level. Groups like Indivisible help you stay informed, show up, and take action with others in your community. Whether it's rallying for voting rights or holding local officials accountable, your voice matters more when it's joined with others.

  • Indivisible Fitchburg MA – get involved locally
  • Third Act - Organizing people over 60 to protect democracy and climate
  • MoveOn – organizes national and local campaigns for progressive change

Support Independent Journalism

A free press is essential to democracy—and it's under threat. Independent outlets expose corruption, debunk disinformation, and tell the stories the mainstream often misses. Subscribing helps them survive and stay accountable to readers, not corporations or billionaires.

Start with these trusted sources:

  • ProPublica – nonprofit newsroom doing deep-dive investigative reporting
  • Mother Jones – fearless reporting on justice, politics, and the environment
  • The Nation – progressive news and analysis with a focus on social justice
  • Rolling Stone – bold political reporting, investigative journalism, and cultural commentary
  • WIRED – covers tech, politics, and the future of democracy with clarity and urgency

Register to Vote—and Help Others Register

Make sure you're registered, then help friends, family, and neighbors do the same. Volunteer for voter registration drives or become a poll worker or poll watcher.

Visit vote.org
